## Institution And Research Context

The listing frames the project around algorithmic transparency, fairness, accountability, and harms from deployed algorithmic systems in domains such as recommendation, social networks, education, healthcare, and law enforcement. The concrete research modes include simulations of algorithmic systems, sock-puppet studies with bots, data-collection tools, and data from browsers and smartphones, with possible real-world impact through collaborations with technical and legal experts in the EU and the US.

Public DTU Compute material presents the department as an applied mathematics and computer science environment with around 400 staff, 10 research sections, and research across mathematics, statistics, computer science, and engineering. It explicitly lists areas such as AI, machine learning, cybersecurity, social networks, fairness, and data ethics.

The Pioneer Centre for AI's Networks and Graphs collaboratory is led by Roberta Sinatra and Sune Lehmann Jørgensen. Its public description emphasises mathematical and physical modelling of networked systems, large-scale experimentation, explainability, novelty detection, and fair AI. Sune Lehmann's DTU profile describes work on quantitative understanding of social systems based on massive data sets, drawing on complex systems, machine learning, and statistical analysis.

Piotr Sapieżyński's public material describes algorithm audits as empirical investigations of deployed ML/AI products, including external audits, web-data collection, reverse engineering web APIs, automated browsing, machine-learning error analysis, statistics, and data visualisation. This is highly aligned with the advert's language around bots, browser/smartphone data, transparency, and accountability.
