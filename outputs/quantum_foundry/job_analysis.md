## Company Context From Listing And Research

Quantum Foundry Copenhagen is owned by the Novo Nordisk Foundation and works on manufacturing processes for next-generation quantum computing processors. The public company material emphasises ultra-high-vacuum manufacturing, atomic and isotopic purity, wafer-based integrated circuits, and superconducting, semiconducting, and photonic components. It also presents the organisation as explicitly interdisciplinary and data-driven, combining mechanical engineering, optical engineering, electrical engineering, material science, quantum physics, and data science.

The foundry is part of the Novo Nordisk Foundation Quantum Computing Programme (NQCP) at the Niels Bohr Institute. NQCP describes its mission as enabling fault-tolerant quantum computing hardware and quantum algorithms for life-science relevant chemical and biological problems. The Novo Nordisk Foundation describes the programme as a DKK 1.5 billion, 12-year effort based at the Niels Bohr Institute.
